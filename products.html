Production Process

1
Retting & Extraction
Flax stems undergo water or dew retting for 2-3 weeks to separate fibers, followed by mechanical extraction.
2
Spinning & Weaving
Long flax fibers are wet-spun for strength, then woven on specialized looms at controlled humidity.
3
Finishing & Calendering
Controlled shrinkage, natural softening, and calendering for desired texture and drape characteristics.

RFD Fabrics

Ready for Dyeing/Printing fabrics that have been pre-treated and scoured to ensure optimal dye absorption and uniform coloration. Unlike raw greige fabric, RFD materials provide consistent results for downstream dyeing and finishing processes.

Quality Advantages

Uniform Dye Uptake
CV% (coefficient of variation) of less than 5% across fabric width and length ensures consistent coloration.
Reduced Dye Consumption
15-20% lower dye requirement compared to untreated greige fabric due to optimized absorbency.
Shorter Processing Time
Eliminates pre-treatment stage, reducing total dyeing cycle time by 30-40%.
